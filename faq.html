About the Tarot

What is the tarot?

The tarot is a deck of 78 cards. It has 4 suits (Wands, Cups, Swords and Coins which also called Pentacles) 56 cards altogether and another group of 22 cards called the Major Arcana which has strange pictures on them with titles like The Magician, The High Priestess, The Lovers or The Moon. It was originally used for playing card games and later they started to be used for divination/cartomancy.

What is the origin of the tarot cards?

The first tarot decks we know of were created in the 15th century in Italy and quickly spread all over europe. They were used to play card games.

When did they start using them for divination / fortune telling?

Only in the 18th century. The deck they started to use for divination was called the Tarot of Marseilles. Later A. E. Waite designed and published the deck which is now known as the Rider-Waite-Smith deck. This was created for divination and is the most popular tarot deck today. Later from the late 80s and 90s a lot of new tarot cecks were created mainly for divination. Many of these are beautiful art objects.

About Tarot Readings

How does a tarot reading look like?

The client asks a question. The reader shuffles the cards and picks a number of cards and arranges them in a certain pattern. This is called a card spread. Then they interpret the cards. The cards have their own meaning and the place they occupy in the card spread also has a meaning. The two together gives insights into different aspects of the client's question. Looking at all the cards the reader can look at the client's question from several directions and can give advice and help the client find solutions to their problem.

How do the tarot cards work during a reading?

Many people talk about mystical energies at work which makes you pick the right cards during a reading. In think nobody really knows why the cards work but they do. I personally believe in a kind of meaningful coincidence. When you ask a question and randomly pick a card to find and answer, this random card will tell you something about your question and will guide you to find your answer.

Tarot Coaching

What is the difference between fortune telling and tarot coaching?

A fortune teller tries to tell you what the future holds for you. They also usually use a lot of esoteric, mystical sounding words which might not make too much sense to most of us. During a tarot coaching session we look at a question or a situation and see what are the roots of this situation and what steps the client needs to take to get the most desirable outcome or the one they would like to achieve.I use plain, everyday language. No esoteric mumbojumbo. Trying to help you get more clarity in your situation and find your way forward.

Shouldn't I pick the cards myself? Will it work if you pick them instead?

Many tarot readers ask the client to shuffle or pick the cards. Many of them even think that this is necessary for the cards to work. But this is only a myth. I almost never let other people handle my cards and still get accurate results. Since the start of the pandemic many tarot readers started to do online sessions and they realised that they get the exact same accurate tesults if they pick the cards themselves. My experience shows that it does not matter exactly how you pick the cards, you will get the cards you need to get.

Can I ask anything?

In theory yes but I will not read ethycally problematic questions. Like "does my colleague cheat on his wife?"" It is their life, none of our business.

Can I have a general reading?

You can ask me to do a general reading like "what will come up in my life in the next 6 months?" Though you need to know that usually the more general the question the more general the answer as well. You can get the best results if you ask specific questions.

Can you give me some examples what are good questions to ask?

As I said better to ask specific questions. You can ask things like "will I find love in the next year?" or "Is it a good time to start a new business, will it be successful?" but the best questions are not just asking about a situation's outcome but try to ask things like "Why are my relationships all unsuccessful and what can I do to have a lasting relationship?" or "What steps I should take to make my business more successful?". But usually whatever your question I will always try to look at the roots of your problem and the steps you need to take to get the best possible outcome. So you do not have to worry too much about your question. I will try to get the most out of it when I do your reading.
